Foundation

Copy Strategy - Adam Enfroy

Prepared: 2026-05-16 For: Adam Enfroy (@adamenfroy) Scope: Voice, pillars, headline bank, hero variants, the framing AG1 writes to across the bridge VSL, the application-tier landing page, the scorecard surface, the nurture sequences, and the BGE-legacy-alumni migration sequence.

Voice profile

The voice is Adam's own, not AG1's. AG1 writes copy that lands inside Adam's existing register without imitating it. The register reads as:

  • Operator-grounded, results-led
  • Direct, beginner-friendly, jargon-light
  • AI-fluent without AI-hype
  • Numeric ("10k a month", "50k a month", "193K subscribers")
  • Outcome-named, not method-named (the buyer wants the income, not the affiliate model)
  • Beginner-empathetic ("helping beginners reach their first 10k/m" - his own IG bio)
  • Comfortable with "blog", "blogger", "affiliate", "AI workflow", "income", "passive" as primary vocabulary
  • Process-respecting (he ships 80-90 minute masterclasses; he is not afraid of long-form)

Words and phrases that fit: - "the blueprint", "the stack", "the workflow", "the system" - "10k a month", "first 10k", "the next stage" - "AI Income Blueprint", "AI Masterclass", "Blog Growth Engine" - "income lane", "income ladder" - "the bridge", "the install", "the implementation" - "Skool community", "weekly live calls", "Founding Member" - "programmatic", "affiliate", "monetisation"

Words and phrases to avoid (off-voice on this prospect or off-brand for AG1): - Guru-vocabulary clusters (the "10x", "next level", "revolution" register) - Hype-verbs (the manifesting / abundance register) - "Agency", "headcount", "team build" (he and Colin run lean, headcount is not the lever) - Em-dashes anywhere - Triple-adjective lists - "Not just X, but Y" structures - Short-minute call CTAs (off-register for an operator-tier audience)

Brand pillars (the four things the copy keeps returning to)

Pillar 01 - The blueprint is built. The infrastructure on top of it is what compounds. Adam has the Skool community, the masterclass funnel, the YouTube channel, the IG handle. The copy treats the offer stack as already-built and treats the funnel infrastructure (bridge VSL, application tier, nurture sequences) as the lever.

Pillar 02 - The 10k-a-month outcome is the floor, not the ceiling. The Skool tier promises "scale to 10k a month with AI". The application tier is the lane above that outcome, for operators who have already passed 10k and want the next stage. The copy never competes with the Skool tier's outcome promise; it extends past it.

Pillar 03 - Beginner-empathetic top of funnel, operator-empathetic application tier. YouTube and IG and the masterclass funnel speak to beginners. The application tier speaks to operators who have already done the work. The copy lane matches the audience lane at every surface.

Pillar 04 - The team is built. The fulfilment capacity exists. Adam plus Colin Shipp plus the existing AdamEnfroy.com operations team have the capacity to deliver an application tier without Adam becoming the bottleneck on every 1-1 call. The copy treats the team as a fulfilment asset, not a hire-more conversation.

Hero variants (application-tier landing page)

Four heroes. Each leads with the same underlying claim - "the lane above 10k a month" - written four ways for split-testing or for matching the surface (application-tier landing page vs scorecard reveal vs YouTube end-card drop-in vs IG bio-link drop-in).

Hero A - The lane-above claim (default application-tier landing-page hero)

You scaled past 10k a month with AI Income Blueprint. The lane above that outcome is the application tier.

Hero B - The stage claim (data-led variant for the scorecard reveal page)

193K YouTube. 97K IG. Several thousand BGE alumni. Stage 4 is the lane that does not exist yet.

Hero C - The blueprint-to-build claim (the YouTube end-card / IG bio-link drop-in)

The blueprint is built. The team is built. The build-with-you tier above the Skool community is what gets installed.

Hero D - The audit claim (the application-route hero for the high-score scorecard reveal)

You scored ten-k-plus. You are a stage-4 operator. The next move is not another course. It is the build-with-you tier.

The application-tier landing-page sample (andrei170.github.io/adam-enfroy-landing/) leads with Hero A. The hub page (andrei170.github.io/adam-enfroy-hub/) leads with the AG1-introducing variant of Hero A.

Voice for the bridge VSL (5-9 minute, Adam-fronted)

The bridge VSL is Adam's voice direct to camera. AG1 writes the script in Adam's register, Adam delivers it. The structural arc:

  1. Open with the warm-traffic acknowledgment. "If you came from a YouTube video, an Instagram reel, or someone who told you about the Skool community, this is the 5-9 minute version." Acknowledges the channel of arrival without forcing them through the 80-90 minute masterclass first.
  2. Bridge to the Skool outcome. "The AI Income Blueprint community on Skool walks the system from zero to 10k a month with AI. 282 Founding Members are inside as of this week. Weekly live calls. 37 lessons. 15 AI frameworks. The work is built."
  3. Address the warm-traffic objection. The warm YouTube subscriber's objection is not "is this real" (the channel already proved it). The objection is "do I need this when I have your free content". Adam answers that directly.
  4. Specific outcome reference. Reference the published student-results roll: low-four-figures to mid-five-figures per month from named operators inside the community. Eddy Ballasteros, Paul Aroloye, Marcos Isaias, Samantha North, Peter Hoopis (real named alumni, public-surface citable).
  5. Application-tier teaser. "If you are already past 10k a month, there is a separate lane - the build-with-you tier above the Skool community. The scorecard at the end of this video routes you to whichever lane fits your stage."
  6. CTA. Single CTA, Skool community below or scorecard for the operators who are already past 10k. No third option.

Voice for the application-tier landing page

The application-tier landing page is the install version of the lane-above claim. The voice register shifts up from beginner-friendly (the Skool surface) to operator-friendly (the application surface).

  • The buyer is an existing operator. They do not need "what is a blog" or "what is affiliate marketing". The copy assumes they already passed the Skool-tier outcome.
  • The buyer's objection is "does Adam personally work on my account, or is this another community". The copy answers that directly: Adam + Colin + the AdamEnfroy.com team, application-gated, cycle-capped.
  • The buyer's second objection is "what does this cost". The copy hint-ranges only, no exact dollar amounts on the landing page surface (the application form surfaces exact pricing for the cycle).
  • The buyer's third objection is "what if I am not at 10k a month yet". The copy routes them back to the Skool tier explicitly: "if you scored under ten-k-plus on the scorecard, the Skool community is the install lane for your stage. Stay there, finish the work, come back when you cross 10k."

Voice for the scorecard surface

The scorecard is 12 questions, four-segment readout (under 1k, 1k-3k, 3k-10k, 10k-plus). Voice is operator-direct.

  • No quiz-style hype. No "discover your hidden blogger archetype". The questions are revenue-segmenting, traffic-segmenting, and stage-segmenting.
  • Score reveal is a 3-line readout per stage. No 47-page "personalised report". The 3 lines tell the operator their stage and what the next move is.
  • High-score branch (10k-plus) routes to the application form on the same page. No "we will email you the results". The handoff is immediate.

Voice for the nurture sequences

Two parallel sequences. Both written in Adam's register, both signed off "- Adam".

Sequence A (post-masterclass self-segmenting attendees): 5 emails over 7 days. Sequence assumes the attendee already watched the 80-90 minute masterclass and self-identified as "past 10k" or "want done-with-you". Tone is "you already know the work, here is the lane above it".

Sequence B (BGE-legacy migration): 5 emails over 10 days. Sequence assumes the recipient bought BGE in 2020-2024 and may or may not be active. Tone is "you finished BGE, here is what we built for what comes next". Opens with an acknowledgment of the BGE-to-AIB rebrand and an honest "we built this for you" framing.

What the copy never does

  • Never overpromises with multiplier-language or income-transformation framing.
  • Never promises a specific dollar figure on the application tier's outcome.
  • Never names Colin Shipp on a public-facing surface (landing page, scorecard, bridge VSL). Colin is named only on this hub, which is internal-facing to Adam.
  • Never frames AG1 as a competing operator to Colin or the AdamEnfroy.com team. AG1 is the funnel infrastructure layer.
  • Never proposes paid traffic in Phase 1 or Phase 2 copy. Paid traffic is a Phase 4 post-install scope conversation.
  • Never uses em-dashes. Hyphens or full stops or commas only.
  • Never uses guru-vocabulary or hype-verb clusters.
  • Never uses short-minute call CTAs. The application form is "four minutes to fill in". The working session is a "30-minute working session, no pitch, no slides".

What the copy always does

  • Always names the asset stack precisely: AI Income Blueprint on Skool at 747 USD, the 2026 AI Masterclass at incomeblueprint.ai, Blog Growth Engine as the legacy brand, 193K YouTube subscribers, 97K Instagram followers.
  • Always treats the offer as already-built.
  • Always treats the application tier as the lane above the Skool outcome, not in competition with it.
  • Always offers two lanes on every surface: stay in the Skool community (under 10k-plus stage) or apply to the build-with-you tier (10k-plus stage).
  • Always closes with a single CTA per surface. The hub closes with the working-session booking. The application-tier landing closes with the four-minute application form.
