Four layers added underneath the existing assets: - Bridge VSL landing in front of the Skool checkout - Scorecard surface as a primary CTA alongside the Skool checkout - Application form on the 10k-plus branch - Two nurture sequences (post-masterclass self-segment + BGE-legacy migration) routing existing list assets into the application form

The masterclass funnel at incomeblueprint.ai keeps running unchanged for cold-traffic opt-ins. The bridge VSL is for warm traffic that bypasses the masterclass. The scorecard is the second CTA on every surface for past-10k operators.

Phase map

Phase 1 - Days 1 to 3 - Bridge VSL on Skool sales surface

Goal: Convert warm YouTube + IG + podcast-referral traffic into Skool community joins at a meaningfully higher rate than the bare Skool surface.

Deliverables shipped: - Bridge VSL script written (the working version is vsl-preview) - Bridge VSL filmed in Adam's existing YouTube studio - Bridge VSL edited and uploaded - Landing page deployed with bridge VSL embed + Skool checkout below - YouTube end-card updated to point at the bridge landing - IG bio-link router updated - Podcast-referral pages updated - Pixel + GA4 tracking installed and firing - Test traffic confirms the bridge landing renders and the Skool checkout fires

Owner split: - AG1: script, landing page, tracking, router updates - Adam: VSL delivery (Adam-fronted, filmed in his studio) - Colin / AdamEnfroy team: Skool checkout confirmation, end-card update approval

Phase 2 - Days 4 to 7 - Application tier + scorecard + nurture sequences

Goal: Open the lane above the 747 USD Skool community. Route past-10k operators to an application-gated build-with-you tier.

Deliverables shipped: - 12-question AI Blog Income Stage Scorecard live (ScoreApp or Typeform, sample uses Typeform ID uTtFirS4) - Score reveal + 3-line readout per stage + email capture wired - Application form for 10k-plus branch wired with Calendly routing - Application-tier landing page deployed (the working sample on this hub) - 5-touch post-masterclass nurture sequence routing self-segmenting attendees into application lane (Sequence A) - 5-touch BGE-legacy-alumni migration sequence routing historical buyers into application lane (Sequence B) - ESP segmentation logic confirmed (ConvertKit or similar) - Self-serve handoff doc shipped

Owner split: - AG1: scorecard build, application form, landing page, sequences, segmentation logic - Adam: weekly live Skool calls continue unchanged, plus a working-session block per cycle to review application accepts - Colin / AdamEnfroy team: application-tier fulfilment delivery, intake review, cycle management

Phase 3 - Post-install ongoing - Content compound

Goal: Route existing YouTube + IG + blog content traffic into the scorecard and the bridge VSL.

Deliverables (Adam's timeline, not the install clock): - Monthly YouTube end-card update surfacing the scorecard CTA - 1-2 IG posts per week surfacing the scorecard as a stage-check tool - Optional Lane 03 (application-tier authority content) per content-strategy - Quarterly intake review with AG1 to refine application-accept criteria and cycle cadence

Phase 4 - Post-install ongoing - On-brand paid traffic (future phase)

Goal: Scale the warm-traffic conversion lift from Phase 1 + Phase 2 with paid retargeting and on-brand cold traffic.

Deliverables (future-phase scope, not Day-1 install): - Pixel + GA4 + retargeting tags installed and firing during Phase 1 + Phase 2 - Top-performing organic YouTube videos boosted to existing scorecard + bridge VSL surfaces - Retargeting campaigns for bridge VSL landing-page abandons + scorecard quiz starts - Cold campaigns only to on-brand surfaces (scorecard, bridge VSL, masterclass funnel) - Future-phase scope conversation, not Day-1 install

Integration points

  • Skool to bridge VSL landing: the bridge VSL landing routes to the Skool join URL directly. No middleware. Skool handles the checkout, the welcome email, the community access provisioning.
  • Masterclass funnel to nurture Sequence A: the masterclass opt-in form adds a self-segmenting question ("What stage are you at: under 1k a month, 1k-3k, 3k-10k, 10k-plus, or want done-with-you"). The ESP routes the 10k-plus and want-DWY respondents into Sequence A. The under-10k-plus respondents continue on the existing Skool-tier nurture.
  • BGE-legacy list to nurture Sequence B: the historical BGE buyer list (housed in Adam's existing ESP) gets segmented by purchase date and Sequence B is sent to the segment that bought BGE 2020-2024 and is not currently active in the Skool community.
  • Scorecard to application form: the 10k-plus score reveal page surfaces the application form inline (same page, no extra click). The application submission triggers a Calendly link via Typeform / GHL native integration.
  • Calendly to AdamEnfroy.com team: the application working session lands on Adam's calendar (or Colin's, depending on cycle), with the application responses pre-populated in the calendar event description.

Risk + mitigation

Risk Likelihood Mitigation
Bridge VSL conversion rate underperforms the bare Skool surface Low A/B test the bridge VSL landing vs the bare Skool surface for the first 30 days. If conversion is flat, refine the VSL script (Beat 3 and Beat 4 are the highest-leverage tuning points).
Application tier oversells and the cycle cap fills too fast Medium Cycle cap is set by Adam and Colin, not AG1. Tightening the application criteria (raise the floor from 10k-plus to 15k-plus) reduces accept volume per cycle.
BGE-legacy migration sequence creates blowback from inactive buyers Low-medium Sequence B opens with an honest acknowledgment of the BGE-to-AIB rebrand and an unsubscribe-friendly tone. Inactive buyers churn out of the list cleanly; active buyers self-select into the application lane.
Skool tier conversion rate drops because warm traffic now bypasses the masterclass Low The bridge VSL pre-sells the Skool tier at conversion rates equal-to or higher-than the masterclass funnel for warm traffic. Cold traffic continues to flow through the masterclass funnel unchanged. The masterclass funnel does not lose traffic; the bridge adds a parallel lane.
Application-tier fulfilment overwhelms Adam and Colin Medium Cycle cap controls intake volume. The first 1-2 cycles run small (3-5 accepts) to validate fulfilment capacity. Subsequent cycles scale to whatever Adam and Colin can deliver without compromising the Skool community's weekly call cadence.
Scorecard underperforms as a routing tool Low The scorecard is a 12-question quiz with 4 segments. The questions are revenue-segmenting and stage-segmenting, not personality-driven. If routing is muddy, refine the question set in a Day 30 review.
Pixel / GA4 tracking misfires and breaks Phase 4 paid-traffic prep Low AG1 installs tracking during Phase 1 + Phase 2 and validates fires before handoff. Phase 4 paid traffic is a future-phase scope conversation; tracking is in place when that scope opens.

What the funnel does on Day 8

By the end of Day 7, the four layers are live. By Day 8 the funnel is producing:

  • Warm YouTube + IG + podcast-referral traffic landing on the bridge VSL, converting to Skool joins at a meaningfully higher rate than the bare Skool surface
  • Past-10k operators landing on the scorecard, routing into the application form, booking working sessions with Adam or Colin
  • Masterclass attendees self-segmenting on opt-in, with 10k-plus and want-DWY respondents routing into Sequence A and the application tier
  • BGE-legacy buyers receiving Sequence B and routing into the application tier
  • The Skool community continues operating at 282 Founding Members and growing on its existing trajectory plus the warm-traffic lift

By Day 30 the funnel has produced the first application-tier intake cycle, the first measurable bridge-VSL conversion-rate lift, and the first BGE-legacy migration cohort.

By Day 90 the funnel has produced two-to-three application-tier cycles, a stable bridge-VSL conversion-rate baseline, and a cleaned BGE-legacy list segmented into "active reactivation" vs "churn out".
