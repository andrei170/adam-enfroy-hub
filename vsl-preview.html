VSL Preview Script - Adam Enfroy

Prepared: 2026-05-16 For: Adam Enfroy (@adamenfroy) Scope: 90-second sample cut from a public Adam Enfroy YouTube video plus the full 5-9 minute custom bridge VSL spec for Day 1-3 of install.

The bridge VSL sits on a dedicated landing page between cold YouTube / IG / podcast-referral traffic and the AI Income Blueprint Skool checkout. Adam-fronted, talking-head plus b-roll, written in Adam's register, delivered by Adam.

Sample cut (Day 0, the working asset on the application-tier landing page)

The sample on the application-tier landing page (andrei170.github.io/adam-enfroy-landing/) plays a 90-second cut from Adam's flagship YouTube video "How I Make 50k a Month with AI in 2026 (Copy This)" (video ID 0qZuP1E9oSY, oEmbed verified 2026-05-16). This is a public-surface Adam Enfroy video, used as a voice-tone sample only.

Swap to a custom 5-9 minute bridge VSL on Day 1-3 of the install. The sample is for visualisation, not for production deployment.

Full bridge VSL spec (Day 1-3 production asset)

Format: 5-9 minutes, Adam-fronted talking head with b-roll cutaways. Filmed in Adam's existing YouTube studio setup (consistency with the channel the warm traffic just left).

Voice: Adam's own. AG1 writes the script in Adam's register; Adam delivers it. No teleprompter rigidity, the cadence is the same as a flagship YouTube video.

Destination: A dedicated landing page (separate from incomeblueprint.ai/training, which keeps running for cold-traffic masterclass funnel). The bridge VSL landing has the Skool checkout below the video and a scorecard CTA at the end for operators who self-identify as past 10k a month.

Single CTA per stage: - Under 10k a month -> join the Skool community via the checkout below - Past 10k a month -> take the AI Blog Income Stage Scorecard linked at the end of the video

The script arc

Beat 01 - Open with the warm-traffic acknowledgment (00:00 to 00:45)

"If you came here from a YouTube video, an Instagram reel, or someone who told you about the AI Income Blueprint community on Skool, this is the 5-9 minute version. The 80-minute version lives at incomeblueprint.ai if you want the long-form walkthrough. This one is for the operator who already knows enough about what we do and wants to skip to the decision."

Why: The warm YouTube subscriber does not need the 80-minute masterclass. They already watched 30-100 hours of Adam's free content. Forcing them through the masterclass funnel adds friction; the bridge respects the audience's stage.

Beat 02 - Bridge to the Skool outcome (00:45 to 02:00)

"AI Income Blueprint on Skool is the community version of the system I have been walking on YouTube for the last few years. 282 Founding Members are inside as of this week. Eight phases, 37 lessons, 15 AI frameworks. Weekly live calls with me. The price is 747 dollars one time. Founding Member access is locked at that price for the life of the community."

Why: Names the asset precisely. Acknowledges the price openly (the warm subscriber already knows the ballpark; hiding it costs trust). Names the live cadence (weekly calls with Adam) which is the load-bearing differentiator versus the cold "course" alternative.

Beat 03 - Address the warm-traffic objection (02:00 to 03:30)

"The most common question I get from people who already watch the channel is 'do I need this when I have your free content'. The honest answer is, the free content is the why and the what. The Skool community is the how and the timing. The 15 AI frameworks are not on the channel. The 37 lessons are not on the channel. The weekly live calls are not on the channel. If the free content got you 80 percent of the way and the last 20 percent is where you are stuck, the community is where the last 20 percent ships."

Why: Handles the single biggest objection from warm traffic. Does not denigrate the free content (that is the channel's load-bearing acquisition asset). Frames the Skool tier as the implementation lane that the free content cannot be, by design.

Beat 04 - Specific outcome reference (03:30 to 05:30)

"To give you a sense of what the community produces, eleven of our students have published case-study results on adamenfroy dot com. Eddy Ballasteros, Paul Aroloye, Marcos Isaias, Samantha North, Peter Hoopis, and a handful of others. The income range across the case studies sits in low-four-figures to mid-five-figures a month, from a single blog, built using the AI workflow the community walks. Not every operator hits those numbers, and not every operator hits them on the same timeline. The point of naming them is, the system is doing the work in the field, with real operators putting real numbers on the page."

Why: Public-surface citable proof. Real names from the existing published roll. Honest qualifier on outcome variability. This is the section that converts warm-traffic skepticism.

Beat 05 - Application-tier teaser (05:30 to 07:00)

"There is a second lane I should mention. If you are already past 10k a month, the Skool community is below your stage. We built a separate tier for operators who already finished BGE or AI Income Blueprint and want a build-with-you implementation. Programmatic SEO at scale, AI workflow industrialisation, paid traffic, the productised consultancy build. Application-gated. Cycle-capped. We accept a handful per cycle. The scorecard at the end of this video routes you to whichever lane fits your stage. If you scored 10k-plus, the application form opens for you on the next page. If you scored under 10k-plus, the Skool community is the install lane for your stage."

Why: Surfaces the application tier without competing with the Skool tier. Routes the 10k-plus operator into the lane that fits without forcing them to buy the Skool tier first. Two-lane copy keeps the warm traffic on the right side of the funnel.

Beat 06 - Single CTA per stage (07:00 to 08:30)

"The decision is simple. If you are under 10k a month, the Skool community checkout is below this video, 747 dollars one time, Founding Member access for life. If you are past 10k a month, take the 12-question scorecard linked at the end. The scorecard route puts you on the application form for the build-with-you tier. Either way, the next step is one click below."

Why: Two-lane CTA, no third option. Skool checkout for the under-10k stage, scorecard for the past-10k stage. Removes decision friction.

Beat 07 - Close (08:30 to 09:00)

"I will see you inside the community or on an application call. Either way, the system works. Pick the lane that fits your stage and let's get to work."

Why: Direct sign-off. No false urgency. No "this offer expires in 24 hours" pressure. The bridge VSL is evergreen, the Skool community is open-enrolment, the application tier is cycle-capped (urgency lives at the application surface, not the bridge).

B-roll spec (cutaways during talking-head delivery)

  • Beat 01: opens with a static talking-head shot, no b-roll (eye contact with the warm-traffic viewer)
  • Beat 02: cut to a screen recording of the Skool community surface (282 members visible, weekly live call schedule, lesson grid)
  • Beat 03: cut to a screen recording of an AI workflow framework (one of the 15) running live
  • Beat 04: cut to the published student-case-study page on adamenfroy.com, then a quick montage of named student logos / blog screenshots
  • Beat 05: cut to a mock-up of the application-tier landing page (or the live page once deployed)
  • Beat 06: back to talking head for the CTA
  • Beat 07: talking head, sign-off

Production notes

  • Film in Adam's existing YouTube studio. Same lighting, same camera, same mic, same backdrop. Consistency with the channel the warm traffic just left is load-bearing.
  • Wardrobe: Adam's standard YouTube wardrobe (no business-casual upgrade, no tie). The warm subscriber's pattern-recognition is "this is the same Adam I watch on YouTube".
  • Music: minimal or none. The flagship YouTube videos run music-light; the bridge VSL should match.
  • Pacing: same as a flagship YouTube video. 130-150 words per minute. No infomercial cadence.
  • Captions: open captions burnt in (~30 percent of the YouTube audience watches muted on mobile).

Why this is a 5-9 minute bridge and not a 30-minute VSL

  • The masterclass funnel at incomeblueprint.ai is the long-form pre-sell for cold traffic. That funnel does the 80-90 minute work.
  • The bridge VSL is for warm traffic that does not need the long-form pre-sell. Warm traffic that watched a flagship YouTube video already received the equivalent of a 30-minute VSL across multiple Adam videos before clicking through.
  • A 30-minute VSL on warm traffic over-sells and depresses conversion. The bridge respects the audience's stage.
  • A 5-9 minute bridge plus the Skool checkout below converts the warm subscriber at a meaningfully higher rate than either (a) the raw Skool surface with no warm-up or (b) the 80-90 minute masterclass forced on top of warm traffic.

  • Bio-link router updated (YouTube end-card, IG bio link, podcast-referral pages)
  • Test traffic confirms the bridge landing renders mobile + desktop and the Skool checkout fires
  • Bridge VSL goes live by end of Day 3
