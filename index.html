Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the business. The AI Income Blueprint is fulfilling and selling. YouTube is shipping weekly. The Skool community is running with weekly coaching. Adding a 90-day VSL build, a consolidated TOFU page, a premium Cohort tier, and a paid acquisition layer on top of an already-loaded team is how those projects either get postponed by a year or get launched at half the quality. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

One day to film the homepage VSL. One day to film the 7-Figure Blogger Operator Cohort positioning. Calendar holds for cohort delivery in Lever 02. The rest is build team work plus you reviewing the copy, creative, and pricing decisions before they ship. Most operators at your stage spend under 8 hours a month during the 90-day build, and under 2 hours a month after the system is in production.

Will the 7-Figure Blogger Operator Cohort cannibalise the AI Income Blueprint?

No. The Cohort and Blueprint solve different problems. AI Income Blueprint is self-paced curriculum plus Skool community plus weekly coaching for operators earlier in the journey. The Cohort is strategic operating-room access for operators who already cleared the curriculum, hit $5K+/month from a live property, and are ready to scale a single site to $50K-$100K/month. Different deliverable, different audience, different buying motion. Most Cohort applicants will be Blueprint alumni moving up the ladder, not net-new buyers choosing Cohort over Blueprint. Where there's overlap, the Cohort is the explicit "post-Blueprint" tier. It expands the ladder, it doesn't compete inside it.

How does this work alongside the existing Skool community?

Skool stays exactly as it is: the AI Income Blueprint curriculum plus the weekly coaching calls and 4,700+ member community. The Cohort is a senior tier above it, for the slice of Skool members who've outgrown the curriculum and want operator-level peer-room access. Where applicable, Cohort graduates can return to the Skool community as alumni mentors, completing the ladder. The two products complement, they don't overlap.

What if my existing AI Income Blueprint already covers some of what the Cohort would?

There's overlap on some content (AI workflows, content velocity, monetisation), but the deliverable is different. Blueprint is "we teach you the methodology." Cohort is "you've already proven $5K+/month, now we operate at peer-room scale, audit your live property, and scale to $50K-$100K/month with senior strategy." Most Cohort attendees will have already passed through Blueprint and outgrown the curriculum. The overlap is feature, not bug. Cohort buys insider continuity for operators who don't want to start from scratch with another educator.
