Phase 3

Content Strategy - Adam Enfroy

Prepared: 2026-05-16 For: Adam Enfroy (@adamenfroy) Scope: Three content lanes that route YouTube + IG + blog traffic into the bridge VSL and the scorecard. 24-hook bank. Photoshoot brief for the application-tier landing page and the bridge VSL b-roll.

The content engine is already running. The 193K YouTube channel posts on cadence. The 97K IG handle posts on cadence. The adamenfroy.com blog runs organic SEO traffic. What changes after install is the routing layer - where the views go after the play button.

Three content lanes

Lane 01 - Beginner top-of-funnel (no change to current cadence)

The flagship YouTube videos ("How I Make 50k a Month with AI in 2026", income-launch how-tos, AI workflow walkthroughs) and the IG reels (short-form snippets from the channel) continue exactly as scoped today. Adam and the existing content team own this lane.

What changes after install: the YouTube end-card and the IG bio link no longer route to the bare Skool surface. They route to the bridge VSL landing. The 80-90 minute masterclass funnel at incomeblueprint.ai also keeps running for cold-traffic opt-ins. Both routes feed the Skool tier (or, for past-10k operators, the scorecard).

Cadence: unchanged. Adam continues his current YouTube and IG cadence.

Lane 02 - Scorecard-surface content (new, low-volume)

A new content lane that surfaces the AI Blog Income Stage Scorecard as a primary CTA across YouTube end-cards, IG bio-link routers, and a small number of dedicated posts.

  • 1 YouTube post per month with the scorecard CTA in the end-card and pinned comment ("Take the 2-minute AI Blog Income Stage Scorecard - link in description")
  • 1-2 IG posts per week surfacing the scorecard as a stage-check tool for the operator audience ("If you cannot answer what stage you are at in 30 seconds, take the scorecard")
  • 1 LinkedIn post per month for the smaller LinkedIn audience surfacing the scorecard

Why low-volume: the scorecard is a routing tool, not a top-of-funnel acquisition asset. The flagship YouTube channel acquires the audience; the scorecard routes the audience to the right lane. Over-promoting the scorecard cannibalises the Skool checkout funnel.

Lane 03 - Application-tier authority content (new, post-install, optional)

Long-form authority content that speaks directly to the past-10k operator who is the natural application-tier buyer. Two options:

Option A - YouTube secondary channel or playlist. A dedicated playlist on the existing 193K channel ("Stage 4: Past 10k a Month") with 6-12 episodes that walk the operator-tier challenges (programmatic SEO at scale, AI workflow industrialisation, paid-traffic for content sites, productised consultancy build). End-card routes to the scorecard.

Option B - Podcast guest cadence on operator-tier shows. Adam appears on 3-6 operator-tier podcasts per quarter (My First Million, Indie Hackers, The Niche Pursuits Podcast, similar) speaking to the past-10k operator audience. Each appearance ends with a scorecard CTA.

Either option runs on Adam's timeline post-install. Not Day-1 scope.

24-hook bank (working bank for YouTube end-cards, IG hooks, scorecard surface posts, application-tier teasers)

  2. 282 Founding Members are inside AI Income Blueprint as of this week.
  3. Weekly live calls with me. 37 lessons. 15 AI frameworks. 747 dollars one time.
  4. The system that took eleven of our students from zero to low-four-figures to mid-five-figures a month.
  5. AI Income Blueprint is the next-stage version of Blog Growth Engine.
  6. Founding Member access is locked at 747 dollars for life. The price walks up after Founding closes.

Scorecard-surface hooks (Lane 02, route to scorecard):

  1. If you cannot answer what stage your blog is at in 30 seconds, take the scorecard.
  2. The scorecard sorts you into one of four stages. Stage 4 is past 10k a month.
  3. Twelve questions. Two minutes. Routes you to the lane that fits your stage.
  4. Stage 1 to Stage 3: the Skool community is your lane. Stage 4: the application tier is your lane.
  5. The scorecard does not sell you anything. It just tells you which lane is yours.
  6. Your stage in 12 questions. Your next move in 3 lines.

Application-tier hooks (Lane 03, route past-10k operators to application form):

  1. The lane above AI Income Blueprint just opened. Application only.
  2. We built the build-with-you tier for operators who already finished BGE or AI Income Blueprint.

  4. Adam plus Colin plus the AdamEnfroy team. Application-gated. Cycle-capped.
  5. The stage-4 problem is not the system. It is the implementation capacity.
  6. Programmatic SEO at scale, AI workflow industrialisation, paid traffic, productised consultancy. The build-with-you tier.

BGE-legacy-alumni hooks (Sequence B reinforcement, route legacy alumni to migration sequence):

  1. You bought BGE in 2022. The lane above it just opened.
  2. The legacy BGE community plus the next-stage build-with-you.

Cross-platform reinforcement:

  1. The masterclass is the cold pre-sell. The bridge VSL is the warm pre-sell. The scorecard is the stage check. The application form is the lane above.
  2. Four surfaces. One funnel. The lane that fits your stage is one click below.

Photoshoot brief (for the application-tier landing page hero + the bridge VSL b-roll cutaways)

The current public-surface photography on adamenfroy.com is already operator-tone and on-brand. Two new asset categories needed for the install:

Application-tier landing page hero

  • 1 hero shot of Adam in the YouTube studio, eye-line direct to camera, hands on the desk, monitor showing a Skool community surface in background
  • 1 mid-shot of Adam delivering a live Skool call (existing footage from a Skool live call works if available)
  • 1 wide shot of the studio setup (lights + camera + monitor) as a credibility marker

Aesthetic: unchanged from the YouTube channel. Same backdrop, same colour temperature, same wardrobe register. The application-tier buyer's pattern-recognition is "this is the same Adam I watch on YouTube; he is the same operator on a different lane".

Bridge VSL b-roll cutaways

  • Screen recording of the Skool community surface (282 members, lesson grid, weekly live call schedule)
  • Screen recording of an AI workflow framework running live (single framework, 15-30 second clip)
  • Screen recording of the published student-case-study page on adamenfroy.com
  • Montage of named student logos / blog thumbnails (Eddy Ballasteros, Paul Aroloye, Marcos Isaias, Samantha North, Peter Hoopis - all public-surface citable)
  • Screen recording of the application-tier landing page (or mock-up if the page is not yet live)

Source: screen-recorded by Adam or the existing AdamEnfroy.com team. AG1 supplies the b-roll spec and the cutaway timing; Adam supplies the actual footage.

Timeline: Day 1-3 of the install (concurrent with the bridge VSL script approval and filming).

Stock or AI-generated photography

Not used. Demo wireframes ship with gradient or typographic placeholders only. Real photos and screen recordings go in at kickoff. The pattern across the AG1 D100 pipeline is: never fabricate, never AI-generate, real footage only at kickoff (per the standing brief on photography workflow).

What this lane structure ships for Adam

  • Audience does not get redirected. Lane 01 continues exactly as scoped. The 193K YouTube subscribers and the 97K IG followers keep getting the content they signed up for.
  • Routing changes underneath the content. YouTube end-cards, IG bio links, and the scorecard surface route the audience to the lane that fits their stage. Beginner -> Skool. Past 10k -> application tier.
  • No new content production burden. Lane 02 (scorecard-surface posts) is 1-2 IG posts per week and 1 YouTube end-card per month. Lane 03 (application-tier authority) is optional post-install.
  • The team owns the work. Adam and the existing AdamEnfroy.com content team own all three lanes. AG1 supplies the routing layer, the hook bank, the scorecard CTA copy, and the application-tier landing copy. AG1 does not become the content producer.

What this lane structure does not do

  • Does not propose a content rebuild. The flagship YouTube channel and the IG handle stay exactly as scoped today.
  • Does not propose new long-form formats unless Adam wants Lane 03 (optional, post-install).
  • Does not propose paid traffic in Phase 1 or Phase 2. Future-phase paid traffic, if discussed, runs against the scorecard surface and the application-tier landing page, not against cold-stranger acquisition.
  • Does not propose a separate brand or sub-brand for the application tier. The application tier sits inside the AdamEnfroy.com / Blog Growth Engine ecosystem, framed as the lane above AI Income Blueprint.
